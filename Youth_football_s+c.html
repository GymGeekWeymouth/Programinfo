U16
Critical years
The teenage years are not just about growing taller they are one of the most important periods for building the physical foundations a young person will rely on for the rest of their sporting life. Get it right now, and the benefits last a lifetime. Leave it too late, and some of those opportunities simply pass.
Why it matters simply explained
🦴
Bodies are changing fast
During the teenage growth spurt, bones grow quickly but muscles, tendons and coordination take time to catch up. Research from Professors Lloyd & Oliver at Cardiff Metropolitan University shows that well-designed S&C programs help young athletes manage these changes safely, reducing the risk of strains and overuse injuries.
⚡
Strength is trainable at any age
Lloyd & Oliver's internationally recognised Youth Physical Development model shows that strength, speed and power can all be developed safely throughout adolescence provided training is age-appropriate and well supervised.
🏃
Movement skills that last a lifetime
Young people who learn to move well carry those skills into adulthood. The U16 years are a key window to build a solid movement foundation one that makes them better athletes and reduces injury risk for years to come.
🛡️
Fewer injuries on the pitch
Evidence consistently shows that young athletes who follow structured S&C programs are significantly less likely to get injured. For a footballer, this means more time playing, less time on the sideline.
Common myths and the truth
✗ Myth
"Weights will stunt my child's growth"
✓ Fact
No evidence supports this. Supervised resistance training is safe and beneficial for young athletes.
✗ Myth
"Sport practice is enough"
✓ Fact
Sport develops skill. S&C builds the physical platform that makes those skills possible and sustainable.
Grounded in leading research
"Most, if not all, components of fitness are trainable throughout childhood and adolescence the key is age-appropriate, well-structured training that respects individual differences in growth and maturation."
Lloyd & Oliver · Youth Physical Development Model, 2012 & Strength and Conditioning for Young Athletes, 3rd Ed.
Our programs are built on this body of work. We treat each young person as an individual not just their age and design training that meets them where they are physically.
What a session looks like
⚡
Plyometric warm-up
A dynamic, jump and bound-based warm-up that gets the nervous system firing and teaches the body to produce and absorb force.
🏃
Sprint mechanics in and outdoors
Posture, arm drive, stride mechanics and acceleration work that translates directly onto the pitch.
🎯
Movement pattern work
How to squat safely, hinge properly, land without jarring joints, and change direction under control the foundations everything else is built on.
🏋️
Learning to lift safely and properly
Weights introduced progressively with full coaching support. Lifting is a skill done right, it's one of the best investments a young athlete can make.
🏆
Game to finish
A competitive, game-based activity that brings everything together fun, fast-paced, and directly connected to their sport.
